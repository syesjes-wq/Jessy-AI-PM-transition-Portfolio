# Decision Log

This document captures the major decisions made during development of the Chess Progress Tracker.

## Assumption #1: Finding Extraction

| ID   | Observation                                                                                                                                                                 | Root Cause                                                                                                          | Decision                                                                                                    |
| ---- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| D001 | Stockfish provides move-level annotations such as blunders, mistakes, and accuracy scores, but the product requires parent-facing findings and recurring learning patterns. | Stockfish evaluates individual moves rather than higher-level learning patterns.                                    | Stockfish could not serve as the primary benchmark for finding correctness.                                 |
| D002 | No shared definition of a valid beginner-level finding existed.                                                                                                             | Beginner chess findings lacked a structured reference model.                                                        | Create a Finding Taxonomy using beginner chess learning material.                                           |
| D003 | Fewer than 10% of generated findings mapped directly to taxonomy entries.                                                                                                   | Findings were often more specific, combined multiple concepts, or used different wording than taxonomy definitions. | Retain taxonomy as a reference artifact, but abandon taxonomy matching as the primary evaluation mechanism. |
| D004 | Human reviewers could evaluate findings even when direct taxonomy matches did not exist.                                                                                    | Finding correctness depended on evidence and game context rather than exact taxonomy alignment.                     | Introduce an LLM-as-Judge approach for finding evaluation.                                                  |
| D005 | Manual reviews produced inconsistent interpretations of claims, evidence, and chess terminology.                                                                            | Evaluation relied heavily on evaluator discretion.                                                                  | Create a formal Finding Evaluation Framework.                                                               |
| D006 | 18 of 47 findings (38.3%) required escalation to PGN analysis despite ultimately being correct.                                                                             | Finding correctness and evidence sufficiency were being treated as the same quality dimension.                      | Separate Evidence Verification from Finding Validation.                                                     |
| D007 | 8 of 36 evaluated findings (22%) contained Evidence-to-Claim Conversion failures.                                                                                           | The evaluator extracted claims from supporting evidence rather than from the finding itself.                        | Treat evaluator failures as framework design problems and refine the framework accordingly.                 |
| D008 | Evaluators occasionally reached conclusions from incomplete evidence.                                                                                                       | The evaluation process allowed inference when verification was incomplete.                                          | Require verification over inference and introduce explicit escalation rules.                                |

## Assumption #2: Concept Mapping

| ID   | Observation                                                                                                                         | Root Cause                                                                                              | Decision                                                                                                                                                            |
| ---- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| D009 | Open-ended concept generation produced inconsistent labels and varying levels of abstraction.                                       | The system was allowed to generate arbitrary conceptual interpretations.                                | Restrict concept mapping to recognized textbook chess concepts.                                                                                                     |
| D010 | Concepts alone often failed to explain why a mapping was selected.                                                                  | The relationship between findings and concepts lacked an intermediate instructional layer.              | Introduce Sub-Principles as an explicit layer between findings and concepts.                                                                                        |
| D011 | Multiple plausible concepts frequently existed for the same finding.                                                                | Direct concept generation obscured the reasoning process and made concept selection difficult to audit. | Replace direct concept generation with a structured concept-mapping workflow consisting of interpretation, candidate generation, evaluation, and selection.         |
| D012 | Evaluators sometimes anchored on piece identity rather than the behavior represented by the finding.                                | Concept evaluation began before identifying the underlying instructional behavior.                      | Require independent behavior identification before evaluating the AI-generated concept mapping.                                                                     |
| D013 | Generic concepts such as Piece Safety and Piece Activity were sometimes too broad to represent piece-specific instructional themes. | The concept hierarchy did not explicitly define when piece-specific concepts should be used.            | Introduce piece-specific concepts (e.g., Queen Safety, Rook Activity) when they represent recognized instructional themes.                                          |
| D014 | Several mappings initially classified as incorrect were later determined to be valid after deeper analysis.                         | Evaluation outcomes depended heavily on how the judge interpreted behavior, hierarchy, and specificity. | Create a dedicated Concept Mapping Evaluation Framework incorporating behavior-first evaluation, hierarchy validation, specificity checks, and textbook validation. |
